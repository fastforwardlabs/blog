The Danger and Promise of Adversarial Samples
Adversarial samples are inputs designed to fool a model: they are inputs created by applying perturbations to example inputs in the dataset such that the perturbed inputs result in the model outputting an incorrect answer with high confidence. Often, perturbations are so small that they are imperceptible to the human eye — they are inconspicuous. Adversarial samples are a concern in a world where algorithms make decisions that affect lives: imagine an imperceptibly altered stop sign that the otherwise high-accuracy image recongnition algorithm of a self-driving car misclassifies as a toilet.
